Syracuse city schools are ranked the 13th most segregated in the nation according to a study conducted by the Century Foundation. The I-81 highway in Syracuse has served as a physical barrier between high-income white families and low-income families of color since its construction in 1959. This separation has extended into the cityâ€™s school system, separating poor families of color from wealthy, white families who choose to send their children to private or suburban schools. Data from the U.S. Department of Education Civil Rights Data Collection suggests students are further segregated within the Syracuse City School District (SCSD) specifically within Advanced Placement classes.
